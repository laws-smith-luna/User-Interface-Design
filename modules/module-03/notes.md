# Module 3: User-Centered Design

## The Double Diamond Model

A design process framework with four phases, organized into two "diamonds":

### Diamond 1: Finding the Right Problem

1. **Diverge** - Question the problem, expand scope, discover fundamental issues
2. **Converge** - Narrow down and converge on the real problem

### Diamond 2: Finding the Right Solution

3. **Diverge** - Expand possible solutions, explore alternatives
4. **Converge** - Narrow down and converge on the best solution

### Key Insight

Don't jump straight to solving — first make sure you're solving the **right problem**. Each diamond has a divergence phase (broaden thinking) followed by a convergence phase (focus down).

## The Design Council's Framework (Expanded Double Diamond)

*Source: The Design Council (CC BY 4.0)*

Expands the Double Diamond into a full ecosystem with four named phases and surrounding support structures.

### The Four Phases

| Phase | Diamond | Mode | Activity |
|-------|---------|------|----------|
| **Discover** | 1 | Diverge | Explore the challenge, gather insights |
| **Define** | 1 | Converge | Synthesize findings, frame the problem |
| **Develop** | 2 | Diverge | Co-create, prototype, test possible solutions |
| **Deliver** | 2 | Converge | Finalize, test, and launch the solution |

Flow: **Challenge** → Discover → Define → Develop → Deliver → **Outcome**

### Design Principles (Core Values)

1. **Be People Centered**
2. **Communicate** (Visually & Inclusively)
3. **Collaborate & Co-Create**
4. **Iterate, Iterate, Iterate**

### Supporting Layers

- **Engagement** (top) - Connecting the dots and building relationships between different citizens, stakeholders, and partners
- **Leadership** (bottom) - Creating the conditions that allow innovation, including culture change, skills, and mindset
- **Methods Bank** - Tools to Explore, Shape, and Build throughout the process

### Key Takeaway

The Double Diamond doesn't happen in a vacuum — it requires **engagement** with stakeholders, **leadership** support for innovation, and a **methods bank** of practical techniques. The dashed blue arrows show iteration and feedback loops between phases.

## Needfinding (a.k.a. Design Research)

The goal of needfinding is the process of **identifying, articulating, and understanding the needs of users** in a design context by:

- Using methods to gather **qualitative data**
- Exploring **behaviors, attitudes, aptitudes** of potential and existing users
- Identifying **technical, business, and environmental contexts** (domain)
- Observing **vocabulary and social aspects** of the domain
- Gaining clarity on how **existing products** are used
- Empowering the team with **credibility and authority**, helping inform decisions
