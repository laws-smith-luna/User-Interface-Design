# SWE 632 - User Interface Design and Development

**George Mason University - MS in Computer Science**
**Spring 2026** | Prof. Zhicong Lu (zlu6@gmu.edu)
**Course Dates:** Jan 20, 2026 - May 4, 2026

## Course Objectives
1. Design a UI through user-centered design
2. Conduct heuristic evaluations and think-aloud usability studies
3. Use principles from visual, interaction, and community design
4. Improve UI through iterative design
5. Use modern web technologies to implement a UI

## Required Textbooks
- Norman, D. A. (2013). *The Design of Everyday Things*. Basic Books.
- Krug, S. (2014). *Don't Make Me Think, Revisited* (3rd ed.). New Riders.
- Mullet, K., & Sano, D. (1994). *Designing Visual Interfaces*. Prentice Hall.

## AI Policy & Academic Integrity

**From the syllabus:** "Use of Generative-AI tools should be used following the fundamental principles of the Honor Code. This includes being honest about the use of these tools for submitted work and including citations when using the work of others, whether individual people or Generative-AI tools."

### What's Allowed
- Using AI to help **understand concepts** and course material
- Using AI to **brainstorm ideas** and explore design directions
- Using AI to **review/critique** your own work before submission
- Using AI to help **debug code** or explain errors
- Using AI for **learning** and studying for quizzes/exams

### What Requires Disclosure
- Any AI-generated content included in submissions must be **cited**
- If AI helped write portions of an assignment, **disclose this**
- When in doubt, **ask Prof. Lu** for clarification

### Red Flags - Claude Should Warn You
- Generating complete assignment answers for direct submission
- Writing full discussion posts without your own thinking
- Creating deliverables for **Individual Assignments** (must be your own work)
- Anything for the **Final Exam** (closed-book, no AI allowed)
- Generating content without intent to cite/disclose AI use

### Assignment Types to Watch
| Type | AI Guidance |
|------|-------------|
| Individual Assignments | Your own work - AI can help you learn, not do it for you |
| Group Assignments | Discuss AI use with your group, disclose in submission |
| Discussions | Your own thoughts required - AI can help refine, not generate |
| Quizzes | Open-book but should reflect YOUR understanding |
| Final Exam | **NO AI** - Closed book, one attempt |
